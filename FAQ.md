# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is FreeAgent?

FreeAgent is an active runtime and orchestration workspace for multi‑agent systems, trading bots, and developer tooling. It is **not** a constitutional lane — governance authority resides in the 4‑lane lattice.

## What is the relationship between FreeAgent and Deliberate‑AI‑Ensemble?

Deliberate‑AI‑Ensemble is the public archive and research site. FreeAgent is the implementation workspace. Deliberate‑AI‑Ensemble may publish artifacts and results; FreeAgent is where much of the development and runtime execution happens.

## What is the Nexus Graph?

The Nexus Graph is a visualization and navigation surface for document relationships and verification states. It is **not** a source of constitutional authority. Specification lives in lattice‑facing docs; runtime visualization lives in the WE4Free mesh/public site.

## Is the Compact Phenotype Continuity Gate active?

No. The gate policy and decision machinery exist and are tested, but runtime enforcement is **not enabled**. The feature flag is disabled by default.

## How is governance handled?

All constitutional governance resides in the 4‑lane lattice. FreeAgent’s governance files (AGENTS.md, SAFETY_INVARIANTS.md, etc.) are operational guides only. In case of conflict, lattice rules prevail.

## Can I run the trading bot locally?

Yes. Follow the developer setup in CONTRIBUTING.md and TESTING.md. Note that the bot can trade only in paper‑trading mode by default; live trading requires explicit configuration and caution.

## What about Phase 2 extraction?

Phase 2 is **not authorized** at this time. No extraction, movement, or deletion of additional components should occur without explicit approval.

## Where are the enforcement controls?

See `FREEAGENT_ENFORCEMENT_REALITY_REGISTER.md`. Currently, no controls are marked as ENFORCED because none have runtime call sites and blocked‑failure proofs.

## How do I contribute?

See CONTRIBUTING.md for guidance on issues, pull requests, and review expectations. All contributions must comply with project constraints.

## What is the autonomous constraint discovery protocol?

It is a documented process for discovering, simulating, and proposing candidate constraints from repeated NFMs and delegation‑surface expansion. Candidates are advisory until ratified by the lattice.
