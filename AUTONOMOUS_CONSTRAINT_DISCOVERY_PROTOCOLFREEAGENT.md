# Autonomous Constraint Discovery Protocol (FreeAgent)

**Created:** 2026-04-28T07:19:51-04:00  
**Purpose:** Define how the governance lattice can autonomously discover missing constraints from repeated NFMs and delegation-surface expansion without allowing unratified self-law creation. This is a **documentation-only** protocol. No runtime code, no feature flags, no Phase 2, no authority transfer.

--

## 1. Definitions

- **NFM (Need-for-More):** A recorded failure, edge case, or contradiction observed during operation that indicates an absent or insufficient constraint.
- **Delegation Surface:** The set of externalized capabilities, agents, or services that the system relies on or exposes (e.g., external APIs, mesh relays, authority handoffs).
- **Candidate Constraint:** A proposed rule, invariant, or policy generated by the discovery protocol to close an NFM or constrain the delegation surface.
- **Ratification:** The explicit approval of a candidate constraint by the 4‑lane lattice (constitutional authority). Until ratified, a candidate is non‑binding and non‑enforced.
- **Simulation Run:** A sandboxed execution of candidate constraints against historical traces or synthetic scenarios to estimate effects and detect regressions.
- **Governance Lattice:** The supra‑repository constitutional authority (4‑lane system) that alone can ratify constraints.
- **FreeAgent:** The runtime/orchestration implementation trunk. Not a lane. It may host discovery tooling but never constitutional authority.

--

## 2. Protocol Flow

1. **Observation**  
   - System detects repeated NFM signatures (e.g., via logs, test failures, audit mismatches).  
   - Delegation-surface expansions (new agents, APIs, external services) are catalogued.

2. **Classification**  
   - Each NFM is tagged: safety, liveness, consistency, delegation-risk, or unknown.  
   - Delegation-surface changes are scored for exposure and coupling.

3. **Candidate Generation**  
   - Discovery tooling proposes candidate constraints (invariants, policies, schema rules, delegation gates).  
   - Candidates are expressed as declarative policy fragments with clear scope and rationale.

4. **Simulation**  
   - Candidates are run in isolated simulation against historical traces and synthetic edge cases.  
   - Outcomes recorded: passes, regressions, false positives/negatives, performance impact.

5. **Reporting & Review**  
   - A discovery report is generated listing candidates, simulation outcomes, and risk annotations.  
   - Report is submitted to the governance lattice for review (no automatic enforcement).

6. **Ratification or Rejection**  
   - The lattice may ratify, amend, or reject each candidate.  
   - Only ratified constraints become enforceable and are propagated to runtime policy stores.

7. **Post‑Ratification Integration**  
   - Enforceable policy is versioned and rolled out behind appropriate gates.  
   - Discovery tooling logs the closed NFM and links to the ratified constraint.

--

## 3. Safety Boundaries

- **Discovery ≠ Authority:** The protocol may discover and propose, but never ratify or enforce by itself.
- **Simulation Sandboxing:** Simulation runs must be fully isolated from production state and decision enforcement.
- **No Self‑Law:** The system cannot create, apply, or enforce constraints without explicit lattice ratification.
- **Audit Trail:** All observations, candidate proposals, simulation results, and ratification decisions must be logged immutably.
- **Human‑in‑the‑Loop:** Critical changes require explicit human acknowledgment before ratification.
- **Rollback Readiness:** Any ratified constraint can be suspended or rolled back by the lattice if adverse effects emerge.

--

## 4. Delegation Amplification Theorem (Informative)

> As the delegation surface expands, the number of implicit trust assumptions grows superlinearly. Without explicit constraints, small delegations can amplify into systemic risk.

- **Implication for Discovery:** Repeated delegation expansions increase the probability of undetected NFMs. The protocol should prioritize candidates that constrain or clarify delegation boundaries.
- **Mitigation:** Candidates should require explicit delegation contracts, capability revocation paths, and audit checkpoints before new delegations are accepted.

--

## 5. Evidence Requirements

- **NFM Evidence:** At least two independent occurrences or one reproducible edge case with traceable impact.
- **Candidate Rationale:** Clear mapping from NFM or delegation change to proposed constraint.
- **Simulation Results:** Quantitative outcomes (pass/fail rates, regression metrics) and qualitative notes on false positives/negatives.
- **Impact Analysis:** Estimated operational impact, performance delta, and compatibility considerations.
- **Ratification Record:** Lattice decision, versioned policy artifact, and reference to simulation evidence.

--

## 6. Decision Statuses

| Status | Meaning | Next Action |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Observed** | NFM logged; awaiting classification | Classify and queue for candidate generation |
| **Candidate** | Proposal drafted, not simulated | Schedule simulation run |
| **Simulated** | Simulation complete; outcomes known | Submit report to lattice |
| **Proposed** | Report submitted; awaiting ratification | Lattice review; may request re‑simulation |
| **Ratified** | Constraint approved and versioned | Integrate into enforceable policy store |
| **Rejected** | Constraint rejected | Close NFM as “not actionable” or reopen with revised candidate |
| **Deployed** | Ratified constraint now enforced in production | Monitor for regressions |
| **Suspended/RolledBack** | Constraint temporarily or permanently disabled | Investigate cause; amend or retire |

--

## 7. What Not to Do

- Do **not** allow discovery tooling to automatically enforce candidate constraints.
- Do **not** treat simulated outcomes as proof of safety without human review and lattice ratification.
- Do **not** expand delegation surface without documenting and reviewing delegation contracts.
- Do **not** use discovery to bypass constitutional governance (the 4‑lane lattice).
- Do **not** enable the protocol in production until simulation and review pipelines are hardened.
- Do **not** delete or amend ratified constraints without lattice approval and a rollback plan.
